# 01 — Getting Started

- **Length:** ~90 seconds (don't rush — 2 minutes is fine)
- **Goal:** In one gentle pass, show a first-time user what BibleMarker is for and the five buttons they'll use, so every other video has a home.
- **Audience:** Someone who just opened the app for the very first time and may feel unsure.

## Flow

| On screen | Voiceover |
|-----------|-----------|
| App open on a chapter of scripture (e.g. John 1). Clean, nothing marked yet. Hold here a moment. | "Welcome to BibleMarker. If you've ever studied the Bible with colored pencils and a highlighter, this will feel familiar — it does the same thing, right here on your screen." |
| Slowly drag a finger across one word to highlight it, then let go (don't open any menus yet). | "You mark the words you notice — the ones that repeat, the people, the places — and BibleMarker keeps track of it all for you. Nothing you do here is permanent, and you can't break anything." |
| Point to the row of five buttons along the **bottom** of the screen so all are visible: **Mark**, **Observe**, **Analyze**, **Reference**, **Settings**. Pause on the row. | "Everything lives in these five buttons along the bottom of the screen. Let me show you what each one does — just a few seconds each." |
| Tap **Mark** (✏️). Pause so viewers see it highlight. Show the key word list. | "The first one is **Mark**. This is where you keep your key words — the important words you want to follow through a passage, each with its own color. This is the one you'll use most." |
| Tap **Observe** (🔍). Pause. Show the row of tabs: Lists, People, Places, Time, Flow. | "Next is **Observe**. This is your notebook — where you jot down what you're noticing about the people, the places, the timing, and how the passage flows." |
| Tap **Analyze** (📊). Pause. Show the tabs briefly, don't dwell. | "**Analyze** brings it all together — a big-picture view of the book you're studying, and room to write down what it means to you." |
| Tap **Reference** (📖). Pause. Show the tabs briefly. | "**Reference** is like having study books on the shelf — you can look up related verses and the original Hebrew and Greek words whenever you're curious." |
| Tap **Settings** (⚙️). Pause. Show the tabs: Appearance, Bible, Data, Studies, Help. | "And **Settings** is where you make things comfortable — the text bigger, download Bible translations, and keep a backup of your work so it's always safe." |
| Return to the reading view. Cursor / finger rests gently on the passage. | "That's the whole tour. There's no wrong way to explore — tap around, and if you ever land somewhere unexpected, just tap one of these five buttons to come back. In the next video, we'll mark our very first word together." |

## Notes for the recorder

- Move slowly. After each tap, pause a beat so viewers can find the same button on their own device.
- The reassurance lines ("you can't break anything," "just tap to come back") matter — don't cut them for time.

**A gentle first look at BibleMarker.** If you've studied the Bible with highlighters and colored pencils, this app will feel right at home. In about 90 seconds, see what it's for and the five buttons you'll use.
