# 01 — Getting Started

- **Length:** ~90 seconds
- **Goal:** In one pass, show a new user what BibleMarker is for and the five tabs they'll live in, so every other video has a home.
- **Audience:** Brand-new user who just opened the app for the first time.

## Flow

| On screen | Voiceover |
|-----------|-----------|
| App open on a chapter of scripture (e.g. John 1). Clean, nothing marked yet. | "Welcome to BibleMarker. This is a tool for inductive Bible study — the kind where you observe the text closely, mark what you see, and draw your own conclusions." |
| Slowly highlight a word in the passage, then let it go (don't open menus yet). | "The whole app is built around one habit: read carefully, then mark what stands out." |
| Point to / tap the bottom toolbar so all five tabs are visible: **Mark**, **Observe**, **Analyze**, **Reference**, **Settings**. | "Everything you need is in these five tabs along the bottom. Let's take ten seconds on each." |
| Tap **Mark** (✏️). Show the keyword list panel. | "**Mark** is where you create key words — the repeated words and ideas you want to track. Colors, symbols, the works. That's the heart of the app." |
| Tap **Observe** (🔍). Show the tab row: Lists, People, Places, Time, Flow. | "**Observe** is where you record what you're seeing — your Lists, and the People, Places, Time, and Flow of the passage." |
| Tap **Analyze** (📊). Show the tab row briefly. | "**Analyze** pulls it all together — an overview of the book, a timeline, themes, and space for your interpretation and application." |
| Tap **Reference** (📖). Show the tab row briefly. | "**Reference** is your research desk — cross-references, Strong's numbers, and the original Hebrew and Greek." |
| Tap **Settings** (⚙️). Show the tab row: Appearance, Bible, Data, Studies, Help. | "And **Settings** is where you download translations, back up your work, and sign in to sync across your devices." |
| Return to the reading view. Cursor rests on the passage. | "That's the whole map. Start by marking a key word — we'll cover exactly how in the next video. Happy studying." |

## YouTube description

**A 90-second tour of BibleMarker — the inductive Bible study app.** Learn what the app is for and the five tabs you'll use every day.

In this video:
- What inductive Bible study looks like in BibleMarker
- Mark — create key words with colors and symbols
- Observe — Lists, People, Places, Time, and Flow
- Analyze — book overview, timeline, themes, interpretation
- Reference — cross-references, Strong's, Hebrew & Greek
- Settings — translations, backups, and sync
